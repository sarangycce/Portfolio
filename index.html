My Story

Built on the Factory Floor,
Proven Across OEM Boardrooms

My journey began as a junior electronics engineer at a Tier-1 automotive supplier, where I spent long nights debugging intermittent ECU failures on vehicle integration test benches. Those early years instilled in me a deep respect for automotive-grade reliability standards — and the irreplaceable value of hands-on diagnostic skill.

Over the next decade, I progressed through hardware design, EMI/EMC engineering, and system architecture roles — leading PCB design teams, driving CISPR 25 compliance campaigns, and defining hardware architectures for safety-critical ASIL-D systems. I've sat in APQP reviews, survived PPAP audits, and shepherded projects through to production release across three continents.

As a Principal and then Chief Engineer, I began to see the bigger picture: companies weren't failing because of bad engineers — they were failing because of poor architecture decisions made too early, EMC issues discovered too late, and leadership gaps at critical inflection points. That realization prompted me to found Sarang Gupta Engineering Advisory.

Today, I work directly with CTOs, VP Engineering, and product teams to compress timelines, eliminate technical risk, and build the engineering culture that delivers reliable electronics — on schedule, on budget, and to specification.

EMI/EMC EV Startup Featured

EV Battery Management System: CISPR 25 Compliance Recovery

🔴 The Challenge

An EV startup's BMS controller was failing CISPR 25 Class 5 radiated emissions limits by 18 dB at 150 MHz — six weeks before planned type approval testing. The program had already slipped once and a second delay would have triggered investor review clauses.

🔵 My Approach

I performed a systematic near-field EMI scan to identify the dominant emission source: a PWM switching node coupling through an inadequately stitched copper pour. I redesigned the PCB ground plane topology, added targeted filtering on the CAN bus interface, and specified a revised enclosure aperture gasket. No board respins — all changes implemented via ECO in current fab.

🟢 The Outcome

Post-modification pre-compliance scan showed 22 dB margin to CISPR 25 Class 5 limits. The program passed formal certification on the first attempt — on schedule. Estimated savings: €140,000 in avoided respins and schedule delay costs.

Power Electronics EV Startup

On-Board Charger Power Stage Design — 22 kW AC/DC

Challenge

An EV startup's in-house power electronics team had achieved only 91% efficiency on their OBC prototype — well below the 96% target required for their thermal budget. Additionally, the design was failing IEC 61000-3-2 harmonic limits with significant THD in the AC input current.

Approach

SPICE-based analysis identified excessive switching losses due to hard-switching in the PFC stage. I guided the redesign toward a totem-pole bridgeless PFC topology using GaN FETs, along with a full MATLAB/Simulink control loop model to optimize switching frequency and dead-time control.

Outcome

Second prototype achieved 97.1% peak efficiency. IEC 61000-3-2 harmonic compliance achieved with 14% THD margin. Product launched on schedule.
